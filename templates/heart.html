Cardiovascular (Heart) Disease

Please go through the following if you are unaware of your blood pressure. Otherwise, scroll down to enter the next symptom.

Diastolic pressure is always lower than systolic pressure.
What range of BP should you enter? (Systolic/Diabolic)
High: 180/120 mmHg or higher. Symptoms include: Sweating, Sleeping problems, Anxiety, Headaches, Vision problems, Nose bleeding, Trouble breathing, Fits, and Black-outs etc Click here for more info
Normal: 120/80 mmHg. They usually have minimum or no symptoms mentioned above and below.
Low: Lower than 90/60 mmHg. Symptoms include: Dizziness, Nausea, Lightheadedness, Blurry vision, Confusion, Fainting, Sleepiness, and Weakness. Click here for more info

To enter the next symptom, please read the below.

What option should you choose for cholesterol?
High (240 mg/dL): Symptoms include: Chest pain or discomfort (angina) – especially during physical activity, Shortness of breath, Pain in the neck, jaw, upper abdomen, or back, Swelling or pain in the abdomen,numbness of the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body etc For more info, click here.
Borderline High (200 to 239 mg/dL): The above symptoms may occur very rarely.
Normal (less than 200 mg/dL): No such above mentioned symptoms.

Please read below to proceed further.

What option should you choose for Glucose (Diabetes)?
Diabetic (126 mg/dL (7 mmol/L) or higher): Please go here to check whether you are diabetic or not. Or whether you are prone to diabetes or not.
Pre-Diabetic (100 to 125 mg/dL): Subtle Symptoms include: Increased thirst, Frequent urination, Increased hunger, Fatigue, Blurred vision etc For more info, click here.
Normal (Non-Diabetic, less than 100 mg/dL): You should not have any symptoms mentioned above.
